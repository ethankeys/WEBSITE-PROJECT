Exo-Planets:

An Exo-Planet is any planet out side of our own solar system. For example, Kepler 22-b is considered and exo-planet as it orbits the star, Kepler 22 (The 22 star found by the Kepler telescope.) Kepler 22-b is considered an Earth-Like Planet, which means that it is similar is size, contents, such as elements and may even have liquid water. Earth-like planets like Kepler 22-b are canidates for habitability, in other words, life. Other Earth-like planets are listed too the side, make sure to check them out! Find one you're interested in! If you decide to do so, you will soon come to realize that there are way more out there. We might not even be alone!
Fun Fact: Did you know that their are about 250 billion stars in the Milky Way? Scientists believe that each star may have 1 exo-planet orbiting it! some even have up to 7! Multiply all of those and you will see the possibility! Our galaxy may have over 1 trillion planets! Some may harbor life!

Feel free too search each of them up to gain a better understanding of these planets which are still yet to be excavated.
Each of these planets are circling their star aswell. Notice how each planet is followed by a letter? This is because of the amount of planets around that star!
The planet name is based on the name of the star (Ex Wolf 1061, Kepler 22, Proxima Centauri) Then the letter on its position from the sun!
For example, "a" would be the closest to the star, followed in alphabetical order Using this system we can use our own star, the Sun to theoretically name Earth.
Our solar system has 8 planets, which can be put in the acronym MVEMJSUN to relate to the order. You can even make a little catchphrase for this such as, M(y) V(ery) E(asy) M(ethod) J(oin) S(uzy) U(ntil) N(ighttime).
As you can see, our 'E' in the acronym which stands for "Earth" comes third which can be stated as E=3. The third letter of the alphabet is C so C=3 which is equal to E=C.
Knowing that E=C we now take the star name Sun and add a c to the end. So using this, Earth's name would actually be Sun c in this system.
HOWEVER this name is not used as we have common names for our solar system. Generally this is only used for exo-planets. Which include Earth-Like planets aswell.

Word Defintion(Taken and simplified from Google)
Space a continuous area or expanse which is free, available, or unoccupied.
Astrophysics the branch of astronomy concerned with the physical nature of stars and other celestial bodies, and the application of the laws and theories of physics to the interpretation of astronomical observations. Ps, they generally study for long periods but get paid little in the process.
Astronaut a person who is trained to travel in a spacecraft.
Exo-planet a planet which orbits a star outside the solar system.
Star a fixed luminous point in the night sky which is a large, remote incandescent body like the sun.
Orbit the curved path of a celestial object or spacecraft around a star, planet, or moon, especially a periodic elliptical revolution.

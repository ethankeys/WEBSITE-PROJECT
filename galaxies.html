Spiral Galaxy

A spiral galaxy occurs when the stars and gas clouds are mainly concentrated in one or more spiral arms.
Think of it as a swirl on an ice cream cone. The tip of the ice cream does not connect to the rest of the icecream, but instead it makes a little curl!
Fun fact: Our own galaxy, in which Earth, Our solar system, etc. is located was discovered to be a barred spiral galaxy in the 1960's!

Elliptical Galaxy

An elliptical galaxy is defined as a galaxy who's shape is close to that of an ellipsoidal one.
Basically, these galaxies just look like round oval footballs that are smooth in appearance, this meaning its hard to tell detail from it!
Fun Fact: The galaxy M87 is one of the universe's largest galaxies and it is elliptical!

Ireggular Galaxy

Irregular galaxies are basically the 'mixture' of a spiral and an elliptical in a sense. This is because they have no specified shape.
These galaxies can take any shape which in turn makes them look very messy and all over the place!
Fun Fact: about 20% of all galaxies in the universe are irregular.
